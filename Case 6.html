Product Catalog Management

Product catalog management involves organizing and maintaining product listings to improve searchability and accessibility. Efficient search algorithms play a key role in this process. Below are some of the important algorithms used in managing and searching product catalogs in e-commerce systems:

Algorithms Used in Product Catalog Management

String Matching: String Matching algorithms are used to search for a specific pattern or substring within a larger string. These algorithms, such as Knuth-Morris-Pratt (KMP) or Rabin-Karp, are essential for matching product names, descriptions, or tags in catalogs to customer search queries. In product catalog management, String Matching ensures that customer searches return accurate and relevant results by efficiently scanning through product titles and descriptions for matching terms.

Linear Search: Linear Search is a simple search algorithm that scans each element in a list sequentially to find a match. While it has a time complexity of O(n), it is straightforward and effective for small datasets or unsorted product catalogs. In product catalog management, Linear Search can be used to locate specific items in small or unsorted datasets, such as a limited-time promotional list or recently added products.

Binary Search: Binary Search is an efficient search algorithm that operates on sorted datasets. By repeatedly dividing the search range in half, it reduces the search space exponentially, achieving a time complexity of O(log n). For product catalog management, Binary Search is highly effective when dealing with large, sorted datasets. It enables quick retrieval of products by attributes like name, category, or price, enhancing the user experience by reducing search times.
